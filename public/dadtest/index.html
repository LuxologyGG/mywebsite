Profile

Built on compassion, rigor, and long-view patient care.

Dr. Farjami's path into medicine was profoundly shaped by losing his mother to multiple myeloma as a teenager. That experience continues to inform a practice philosophy grounded in clarity, empathy, and disciplined treatment planning.

He is especially interested in geriatric oncology, where care must account not only for disease biology, but also resilience, quality of life, family context, and the realities of everyday function.

Care philosophy

  • Treat disease effectively while working proactively to reduce side effects.
  • Coordinate care carefully across specialties, facilities, and families.
  • Use patient education as part of treatment, not as an afterthought.
  • Match clinical intensity to the person, not only the diagnosis.

Research profile

Focused on evidence, publications, and better lived outcomes.

Dr. Farjami is affiliated with Translational Research in Oncology (TRIO) at UCLA and has participated as a principal or sub investigator in more than one hundred clinical trials.

His published work spans peer-reviewed journals, abstracts, and presentations, with recurring interest in breast, gastrointestinal, and thoracic oncology.

Recent public updates also reflect a commitment to strengthening ties between community oncology and academic medicine, with a patient-first approach guiding those collaborations.
